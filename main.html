Information about E-Waste

E-waste, short for Electronic Waste, is the ever increasing mountain of dsicarded electronic devices that have reached the end of their useful life. Our insatiable appetitite for the latest tech fuels this problem, as we cast aside old cell phones, computers, televisions, and countless other gadgets. While these devices contain valuable materials like rare earth metals and precious metals, improper disposal poses significant environmental and health threats. Landfills are oevrflowing with e-waste, and these devices often leak toxic substances like lead, mmercury, and flame retardants. This contamination can seep into the soil and water table, harming wildlife and potentially enter the food chain. Furthermore, some recyling methods in developing countries involve crude techniques like open burning, releasing harmful products in the air and posing health risks to workers
